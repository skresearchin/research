Equity Momentum Published 22 May 2026

Adani Power: Technical Structure Update

The stock continues to demonstrate relative strength supported by positive price structure and sector momentum. Sustaining above key support zones may support continuation of the prevailing trend.

Adani Power has held above the higher-timeframe support zone through the recent corrective phase, with weekly closes maintaining a sequence of higher lows. The structure remains constructive as long as the swing-low support holds on a closing basis. A loss of that level would shift the read to a more neutral stance and call for re-evaluation against the broader power-sector RS picture.

On the daily timeframe, momentum readings have rebuilt after the recent base, and price has reclaimed key moving averages with confirming volume. The current structure resembles a continuation pattern within the larger uptrend, where pullbacks are being bought into rather than sold. Relative strength against the NIFTY remains positive across multiple lookback windows, which adds weight to the bullish bias.

The power sector has been one of the leadership pockets in recent months, and Adani Power continues to feature among the higher RS names within the sector. Watching for confirmation through sustained acceptance above the recent breakout shelf, with invalidation on a decisive close back into the prior consolidation range.

Key Takeaway

Trend structure remains intact while support holds. Bias stays constructive above the swing-low; a clean close below invalidates the current read.

Equity Trend Published 19 May 2026

Marico: Trend Observation

The broader trend structure remains constructive with stable momentum characteristics. Price behaviour near breakout zones will remain important for directional confirmation.

Marico is consolidating just below a multi-week resistance shelf, with the daily structure showing a tightening range and shrinking volatility. This kind of compression has historically resolved in the direction of the underlying trend, which here remains positive on the weekly timeframe. A confirmed acceptance above the resistance band, supported by an expansion in volume, would be the cleanest trigger for continuation.

Failure to break out and a rejection back into the prior range would not invalidate the longer-term structure, but would shift the near-term bias to neutral. The key levels to watch are clearly marked on the daily chart and the FMCG sector behaviour adds important context, with sector RS still inside a constructive range.

Key Takeaway

Watch the resistance band for a confirmed breakout. Volume expansion is the deciding input.

Index NIFTY Published 16 May 2026

NIFTY: Structural View

Index structure continues to respect higher-timeframe support zones with momentum holding above key moving averages. Sustained acceptance above near-term levels would extend the existing trend.

The weekly NIFTY chart continues to print higher highs and higher lows, with the most recent pullback finding buyers near the previously broken resistance turned support. Daily momentum has remained constructive, and breadth within the index has stayed supportive of the broader trend. As long as this structure holds, the bias is to use pullbacks as observation points rather than reversal signals.

On the upside, the immediate ceiling sits at the recent all-time high cluster. A weekly close above that zone, accompanied by participation from heavyweights, would open up the next measured-move target. The downside reference point is the weekly swing low, which also coincides with a confluence of moving-average support.

Key Takeaway

Trend remains intact above the weekly swing-low support. Breakout above the all-time high cluster is the next event to watch.

Sector Relative Strength Published 12 May 2026

Sector Rotation: Leadership Update

High relative-strength sectors continue to dominate market participation. Watching for signs of rotation into lagging sectors as part of the broader trend cycle.

The current leadership pattern continues to favour power, capital goods, and select industrial pockets, with daily and weekly relative-strength readings well above the NIFTY benchmark. Defensive sectors have been consolidating without breaking down, suggesting capital is sitting in trending names rather than rotating defensively. This is a typical pattern in mid-cycle trend phases.

Early signs of rotation often show up in the laggards starting to outperform on short-term timeframes before the longer-term ranking shifts. Currently, there are no decisive rotation signals, but a few mid-cap financials are starting to print constructive structure on the daily timeframe. These will be tracked through the next two to three weeks for confirmation.

Key Takeaway

Leadership remains concentrated in trending sectors. No confirmed rotation yet, but early signs in mid-cap financials are worth tracking.

Options NIFTY Published 8 May 2026

NIFTY Options: Volatility Regime Note

Implied volatility has compressed against the prevailing trend, opening up considerations for directional structures with defined risk.

NIFTY implied volatility has drifted back into the lower half of its recent range, which is consistent with the steady trending behaviour of the underlying. In this regime, defined-risk directional structures tend to offer a better expression of view than outright premium selling, because the relative cost of long-premium structures is contained while the directional payoff remains intact.

Positioning in the options chain continues to show meaningful call open interest at the immediate resistance levels and put open interest concentrated near the recent swing-low support, which is typical of a directional-trend environment. Any sharp shift in this open-interest profile would be an early signal of regime change.

Key Takeaway

Low-IV regime favours defined-risk directional structures over outright premium selling while the trend remains intact.

Index Bank NIFTY Published 5 May 2026

Bank NIFTY: Range Behaviour

The financial index is trading inside a well-defined range, with private-bank heavyweights leading and PSU banks consolidating after a strong prior leg.

Bank NIFTY continues to oscillate inside a clear daily range, with the upper boundary marked by recent swing highs and the lower boundary anchored on the 50-day moving average zone. The internal leadership has shifted to private-sector heavyweights, while PSU bank names are consolidating after a strong prior leg. This rotation within the index is keeping the headline level range-bound even as individual components show clean trends.

For now, the working stance is to fade extremes inside the range and wait for a decisive break with volume confirmation before changing the structural read. Any close outside the range on the daily timeframe, paired with broadening internal participation, would be the cleanest signal of a regime change.

Key Takeaway

Range-bound at the index level, trending at the component level. Watching for volume-backed break of the daily range.
